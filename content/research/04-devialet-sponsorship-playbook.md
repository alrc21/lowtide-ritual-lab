## 1. Devialet — Brand & Event Posture

### Positioning
Devialet is a French high-fidelity audio house founded in Paris in 2007. It is engineered around a portfolio of patents (ADH, SAM, ACE) and sells a "French luxury, technically obsessive" story. Phantom — the egg-shaped wireless speaker — is the hero product. Pricing for flagship products sits between **€2,500 and €8,000+**, placing the brand in the same competitive corridor as Bang & Olufsen, KEF Reference, McIntosh and Bowers & Wilkins.

The brand is **not** trying to be a club PA brand. Its positioning is **design object first, audiophile object second, lifestyle artifact third**. Every activation Devialet says yes to has to defend at least two of those three vectors.

### Audience demographics
- 30–55, HNW or aspirational HNW
- Heavily indexed in Paris, London, NYC, LA, Dubai, Seoul, Tokyo, Shanghai, Milan
- Buyers of curated lifestyle brands: Aesop, Hermès, Loewe, Le Labo, Officine Universelle Buly, La Manufacture Cogolin
- Cross-shop with watch collectors, contemporary art collectors, members of private clubs (Soho House, Casa Cipriani, Silencio, Oswalds)
- Strong overlap with the "ambient / contemporary classical / electronic" listener: Nils Frahm, Jon Hopkins, Floating Points, Caterina Barbieri, Suzanne Ciani audiences

### What Devialet cares about
1. **Image control.** Devialet is custodial of the brand. Anything sloppy reflects on the patents and the price tag.
2. **Acoustic stories.** They love any context that lets them say "this room/space sounds like nothing else."
3. **Cultural prestige.** Operas, foundations, museums, biennales, and design fairs convert better than nightclubs.
4. **Hospitality recurrence.** Hotels and members' clubs that bake Devialet into the guest experience are the highest-value channel because the speakers are *in use* every night.
5. **Editorial visibility in the right titles.** Wallpaper*, Monocle, AD, Robb Report, T Magazine, Cereal, Vogue, Numero, Apollo. Tabloid coverage is a negative.
6. **No price erosion.** They do not want to be seen as a "free speaker hire" brand.

### Recent / known event activations and partners
- **Fondation Louis Vuitton, Paris** — Devialet has installed/activated at the Frank Gehry building and partnered around contemporary art programming. **[verify]**
- **Opéra National de Paris (Palais Garnier / Bastille)** — multi-year cultural partnership; Devialet has done patron-circle sound installations and listening sessions. **[verify]**
- **Mandarin Oriental** — in-room and lobby installations across multiple properties, including Paris and Hong Kong. **[verify]**
- **Cheval Blanc Paris (LVMH)** — flagship suite audio.
- **Hôtel de Crillon, Le Royal Monceau, Bulgari Hotels** — luxury hospitality footprint in Europe. **[verify some properties]**
- **Apple retail** — Phantom was sold through Apple stores during the Phantom I era, anchoring the design-object positioning.
- **Huawei** — co-engineering on the Sound X smart speaker, signaling that Devialet licenses its acoustic IP into mass-premium products.
- **Renault, Mercedes-Benz** — automotive sound system partnerships (R5, EQS lines) **[verify exact models]**.
- **Paris Fashion Week / Milan Design Week / Salone del Mobile** — Devialet shows up as an experiential partner for fashion houses and design brands; private listening rooms, dinners, and showroom takeovers.
- **Silencio (Paris members' club, David Lynch)** — ambient/electronic listening culture overlap.
- **Apple Music + Hi-Res Lossless** — co-marketing around lossless playback.

### Pattern
Devialet **lends or installs gear** when the partner brings (a) a real space with acoustic intent, (b) a curated audience, (c) editorial reach in design/lifestyle press, and (d) zero brand-safety risk. They almost never sponsor commercial nightclub events. They lean strongly into **listening rituals, cultural institutions, hospitality, and fashion/design week moments.**

## 2. Comparable Cases From Peers

### Bang & Olufsen
- **Burning Man / desert listening installations** with artists and the Beosound Edge — long-running pattern of B&O lending limited-edition pieces to "tastemaker" experiences.
- **Cercle (the YouTube live-set platform)** has been intermittently associated with high-end audio brands; B&O has placed product in cinematic outdoor sets. **[verify]**
- **Highsnobiety, Dover Street Market, SSENSE pop-ups** — B&O regularly stages listening corners.
- **Saint-Tropez and Mykonos summer programming** — branded sundown sets at hotels (Le Pinet, Cap d'Antibes lineage). **[verify specific properties]**
- *What they ask for:* full creative approval, photography rights, on-product placement at eye level, attendee data when it's a private event.
- *What they give:* gear loan, sometimes a small marketing co-op budget (€5–25k), social amplification from the regional account.

### KEF
- **KEF Music Gallery** flagship stores in London, Hong Kong, Shanghai — host **listening sessions** with artists. Floating Points and Jon Hopkins have done in-store sessions with audiophile demos. **[verify exact artists]**
- **Sound bath / wellness** events at the LS Series flagship in Hong Kong tying into the "Music Gallery" concept.
- **KEF × Lotus Cars, KEF × Marcel Wanders** — design collaborations; brand cares about design-world credibility.
- *Ask:* attendee curation, design-press coverage, premium photography.
- *Give:* venue loan + LS50/Reference loaners for off-site activations, sometimes co-marketing.

### Sonos
- Sonos plays **further down market** but has done strong cultural sponsorships:
- **Sonos × Frieze London / Frieze LA** — listening rooms at the art fair with curated artist programs.
- **Sonos Studio LA (2013–2017)** — proprietary venue with Q-Tip, FlyLo, Solange residencies. The reference for "branded listening venue."
- **Apple Music spatial audio launches** — co-branded events.
- *Ask:* large reach and content output.
- *Give:* often paid sponsorship plus gear, because Sonos competes on volume not exclusivity.

### Genelec
- Pro/studio brand, but increasingly visible in **immersive audio** (Dolby Atmos, ambisonics) installations.
- **Tate Modern, Serpentine, Pompidou** sound art collaborations have used Genelec rigs.
- **Sound bath / meditation / spatial audio festivals** — events like **MUTEK, Rewire, Le Guess Who?, Unsound, Semibreve** have run Genelec-equipped immersive rooms.
- *Ask:* technical credibility, calibration done correctly, artist-driven story.
- *Give:* studio rigs and immersive arrays; not consumer marketing budget.

### Reading the pattern for Lowtide
The brands that lend gear to small, curated ritual/listening series **without** a large fee are KEF, Genelec, and Devialet. B&O wants more reach. Sonos wants paid scale. **Devialet is the right target for an emerging Valencia ritual series**, *if* the production quality matches their lifestyle press positioning.

## 3. What a "Devialet-Tier Event" Looks Like

### Aesthetic
- Architectural venue: cloister, museum atrium, modernist house, Mies-style pavilion, restored industrial space, hotel rooftop, rooftop garden, cellar, brutalist art space.
- Light direction: candlelit, warm tungsten, or pure architectural lighting. **No nightclub LED walls.**
- Materials in shot: linen, travertine, oak, bronze, ceramic. **No vinyl banners.**
- Sound stages designed for **seated or reclining listening** for at least one part of the night.

### Attendee profile
- 80–250 guests, RSVP only, curated guest list with a meaningful percentage of cultural operators (gallery directors, designers, hoteliers, chefs, editors), creators, and HNW residents/tourists.
- Dress code implicit, not enforced, but skews tailored.
- Phones welcome but not the focus — **content output is from a professional team, not from guests' iPhones.**

### Press visibility
- One or two design/lifestyle publications attached as media partners (e.g., **Apartamento, Cereal, Wallpaper*, Plaza Uomo, Monocle, Numero, Vogue España, Neo2**).
- Aftermovie at editorial quality (4K, color-graded, not a quick Instagram cut).
- Photography: a single named photographer, not a team of phones.

### Production quality
- Acoustic measurement of the space before the event.
- Speaker placement signed off by an engineer; not just "set up the Phantoms in a circle."
- Bar program curated (low-intervention wines, an interesting non-alcoholic option, not Red Bull).
- Scent design — Cire Trudon, Diptyque, or a local atelier.
- Print artifacts: a printed program or zine with track list, credits, and a manifesto. (Devialet team takes one home — this is your trojan horse.)

### Run-of-show benchmark
1. **Doors + arrival** (45 min): ambient tracks, low BPM, low volume, drinks.
2. **Listening session** (45–60 min): seated, structured. One guest artist + Lowtide. Phantoms positioned for full-room imaging. This is the moment Devialet is paying for.
3. **Conversation / pause** (20–30 min): drinks, social mingling, scent shift, light shift.
4. **DJ set** (90–120 min): downtempo / organic house / leftfield, never peak-time techno.
5. **Soft close** (15 min): acoustic outro, candles down.

### Concrete benchmarks Lowtide should study
- **Le Sucre rooftop sessions (Lyon)** — for Iberian rooftop ritual format.
- **Despacio (James Murphy + 2manydjs sound system)** — gold standard for "the room is the experience."
- **MUTEK Barcelona / Sónar+D** — for credibility within the Iberian electronic ecosystem.
- **Cercle x architectural locations** — for camera language.
- **Spiritland (London)** — listening-bar reference; closest commercial cousin.
- **Mioko / Tangram / La Bellota Listening Bar (Madrid/Barcelona)** — Iberian listening-bar peers.
- **Le Bain at The Standard High Line** — hotel rooftop ritual format.
- **Off-Sónar curated rooftops in Barcelona** — calibration for what works in Spain.
- **Hôtel Amour / Hôtel Grand Pigalle "soirées"** — for editorial framing.

The single most useful reference: study **Despacio, Spiritland, and Cercle** and combine their sensibilities with a Valencia / Mediterranean vocabulary (sea, salt, terracotta, dusk).

## 4. Sponsorship Deck Structure (10 slides)

Keep it 10 slides. Devialet's brand and partnerships team reads 10 decks a day; they reward editorial restraint.

**Slide 1 — Cover.** Single hero image (your best night), the line "Lowtide Ritual × Devialet — A Listening Series, Valencia & Beyond," and your name + contact.

**Slide 2 — Story.** One paragraph. Lowtide is a Valencia-born listening ritual built around dusk, the Mediterranean, and high-fidelity sound. Frame it as a *ritual* not a *party*. Use the words "listening," "dusk," "ritual," "fidelity."

**Slide 3 — Why Devialet, Why Now.** One paragraph showing you understand their brand: "Devialet's pursuit of sound fidelity is best expressed in spaces designed for intentional listening. Lowtide builds those spaces in Spain's most editorially undercovered cultural city." This reframes Valencia as an *opportunity*, not a B-tier market.

**Slide 4 — Audience.** Numbers + qualitative profile. Instagram followers, average attendance, demographic split, % international, % HNW indicators (hotel guests, members' club crossover). If you don't have data yet, give qualitative archetypes (3 personas).

**Slide 5 — Past events (proof).** Grid of 6–9 photographs from your strongest nights. Caption each with: venue, date, attendance, headline guest. **No bad photos.** If a night doesn't have editorial-grade images, leave it out.

**Slide 6 — Vision 2026.** A 4-event flagship series. Name each one (e.g., *Equinox*, *Salar*, *Vespertina*, *Marea*). For each: location, date window, capacity, guest artist target, partner venue.

**Slide 7 — Activation Proposal.** This is the meat. Three tiers (see §5). Specify exactly what the Devialet rig does in the space: number of Phantoms, calibration, listening-room moment. Include a render or a mood image of the rig in situ.

**Slide 8 — KPIs we commit to.** See §4 detail below.

**Slide 9 — What we ask.** Equipment loan (specify quantity and SKUs), one Devialet engineer for setup on event day, optional co-marketing post on @devialet (one Reel per event). State clearly that you are **not asking for cash** in v1.

**Slide 10 — Team & Contact.** Founder, music director, production lead, media partner. Photo, one-line bio, phone, email.

### What to commit to (KPIs)
- **Attendance** — guaranteed minimum (e.g., 150 RSVP-confirmed) with a curated guest list shared 72h before doors. Quality > quantity.
- **Content output** — 1 aftermovie (60–90s, editorial cut), 1 photo set (30–40 selects), 3 Reels, 6 stories per event. Delivered within 10 days. Devialet gets full usage rights.
- **Press** — minimum 1 tier-1 lifestyle/design publication covering each flagship (Neo2, Vein, Plaza Uomo, Vogue España, Apartamento, Wallpaper* digital). Commit to *placements pursued*, not *placements landed*, but always land at least one.
- **On-site brand presence** — Devialet logo on printed program, a tasteful credit ("Sound by Devialet") in the venue, mention by host at the listening segment.
- **Database** — share anonymized RSVP data and attendee NPS post-event.
- **Audio quality** — independent acoustic measurement and calibration sign-off pre-event.

### What to NOT promise
- Don't promise sales/conversion numbers — you can't track them and Devialet doesn't want to be measured that way at this tier.
- Don't promise tier-1 international press (NYT, FT, Vogue print) — overpromising is the #1 reason brands pull out.
- Don't promise a specific number of HNW attendees — promise a curated list, not a wallet count.
- Don't promise exclusivity beyond Valencia / your specific format. Devialet won't sign a regional exclusive for an unproven series.
- Don't promise UGC volume — Devialet doesn't care about TikToks of strangers; they care about controlled editorial output.

## 5. Deal Structures — Pros, Cons, Recommendation

### A. Equipment loan only (the offered baseline)
- **Pros:** zero cash exposure for either side, fastest to close, lowest risk for Devialet.
- **Cons:** Devialet has no skin in the game beyond logistics; you carry insurance and transport; no co-marketing leverage.
- **When to choose:** event 1 and event 2 only — proof phase.

### B. Equipment loan + content rights
- **Pros:** Devialet gets editorial assets they can repost; you get implicit brand endorsement (a Devialet repost is worth more than a paid post). Aligns incentives — they'll send a better engineer, want better photos.
- **Cons:** you must hire a real production team (photo + video). Cost: €2–4k per event. You eat this.
- **When to choose:** events 2–4. **This is what you actually want.**

### C. Paid partnership (cash + gear)
- **Pros:** you get a budget (typical range €10–40k for a series at this tier); allows you to upgrade venue, talent, hospitality.
- **Cons:** Devialet's expectations rise sharply. They'll want approvals on every asset, audience guarantees, and probably a media plan. You become a vendor, not a peer.
- **When to choose:** only after 3 successful events and a measurable audience. Year 2 conversation.

### D. Revenue share on a flagship series
- **Pros:** aligns long-term; if you build a true flagship (think *Lowtide Sessions* as a recurring branded property), you can argue for a share of bar/ticket/merch revenue.
- **Cons:** Devialet's commercial team isn't built for this — they're a hardware company. You'll spend 6 months negotiating with the wrong department.
- **When to choose:** probably never with Devialet. Better fit with a hotel group or beverage sponsor.

### Recommended path
**v1 (events 1–2): A.** Loan only. Prove the format. Capture editorial.
**v2 (events 3–4): B.** Loan + content rights. Use the assets to upsell internally at Devialet.
**v3 (year 2): C.** Paid series. Pitch *Lowtide × Devialet* as a 4-city Iberian series (Valencia, Ibiza, Madrid, Lisbon).

Be opinionated in the meeting: tell them you want path A now and path B by event 3. Brands respect operators who know their stage.

## 6. Risk Register

| # | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| 1 | Bad press / nightlife incident (intoxication, neighbor noise complaint, fight) | Medium | Catastrophic | Curated guest list, security, end ambient by midnight, written venue agreement, no open bar formats |
| 2 | Production looks "small" next to Devialet rig | High at event 1 | High | Hire a real lighting designer + scenographer for event 1. Cut budget elsewhere if needed. |
| 3 | Crowd mismatch (techno crowd at a listening event) | Medium | High | Apply-only RSVP, no public ticket links, founder reviews list, vibe-curate the first 50 attendees personally |
| 4 | Damage to Phantoms | Low | Medium-High | Insurance policy on the loan (specify in MoU), no speakers within 2m of standing crowd, raised plinths |
| 5 | Missed content deliverables | Medium | High | Hire one named director/photographer per event with a contractual delivery date and partial payment on delivery |
| 6 | Press doesn't materialize | High | Medium | Pre-pitch 4 outlets per event, secure 1 confirmed before the event; have a media partner attached from event 1 |
| 7 | Devialet engineer not assigned, you bluff calibration | Medium | High | Hire a freelance acoustic engineer in Valencia as backup; never improvise on the rig |
| 8 | You scale too fast (Ibiza event 3) and quality drops | High | Catastrophic for renewal | Cap at 4 events year 1, all in Valencia or one curated outpost |
| 9 | Competitor brand (B&O, KEF) co-appears | Low | Medium | Negotiate category exclusivity per event in the MoU |
| 10 | Internal Devialet champion leaves | Medium | High | Always have two contacts inside Devialet (marketing + hospitality/B2B) |

## Implications for Lowtide — 30 / 60 / 90 Day Plan

### Days 0–30 — Get the deal closeable
- [ ] Lock the **MoU draft** for option A (loan only, 1 event, written terms): insurance, transport, calibration, brand usage, content rights, exclusivity scope.
- [ ] Build the 10-slide deck. Hire a designer for one weekend if needed (€500–1,000). The deck must look like a Wallpaper* feature, not a pitch deck.
- [ ] Choose the **flagship venue for event 1.** Targets in Valencia: Bombas Gens (industrial art space), Veles e Vents (David Chipperfield, port), a cloister in El Carmen, a private modernist villa in El Saler. Site visit + photos.
- [ ] Sign a **media partner** for event 1. First call: *Neo2*, *Vein*, *Apartamento* (HQ Barcelona), *Plaza Uomo*. One yes is enough.
- [ ] Hire **one named director/photographer** for the year. Show Devialet the reel in the meeting.
- [ ] Build a **300-name curated guest list** (gallery directors, hoteliers, designers, chefs, members of Soho House Barcelona, AD/Vogue España contributors).
- [ ] Pitch meeting with Devialet — present deck, propose option A for event 1, signal option B from event 2.

### Days 30–60 — Execute event 1 at Devialet standard
- [ ] Confirm gear list, delivery, calibration day. Pay for an acoustic engineer if Devialet doesn't send one.
- [ ] Production: lighting designer, scent design, printed program/zine, bar program with one named wine/spirit partner, soft seating for the listening segment.
- [ ] Confirm guest artist. Aim for someone with editorial credibility (an Erased Tapes / Leiter / Modern Recordings type, or a Spanish equivalent like **Pional, John Talabot, Marc Piñol**).
- [ ] Pre-event content plan: 3 teaser posts, 1 Reel, 1 newsletter blast.
- [ ] **Run the event.** Listening segment is non-negotiable.
- [ ] Same-night content capture; deliver edit within 7 days.

### Days 60–90 — Convert the proof into a series
- [ ] Send Devialet a **post-event report**: photos, video, attendee profile breakdown, press placements, NPS, three quotes from attendees, one recommendation.
- [ ] Schedule the **renewal conversation within 14 days of the event.** Pitch option B (loan + content rights) for events 2–4 as a season.
- [ ] Draft the **2026 series calendar**: 4 events, named, with venues optioned. Equinox (March), Vespertina (June), Salar (September), Marea (December).
- [ ] Open a parallel conversation with **one hotel partner** (Sercotel, Caro Hotel, Only YOU, Hospes Palau de la Mar, or push for Mandarin Oriental Madrid) — Devialet loves hotels; co-locating raises the ceiling.
- [ ] Begin **press relations 90 days out** for event 2.
- [ ] Build the **Lowtide x Devialet microsite** (single page, lookbook style) — this is the artifact Devialet's marketing team will share internally to get budget approved for v3.

### The single thing that decides the partnership
Devialet doesn't decide based on the deck. They decide based on whether **event 1 looks like something they would have produced themselves.** Spend disproportionately on production quality at event 1 — even if it loses money. Everything after that is easy.
