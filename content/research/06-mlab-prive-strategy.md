# Mlab × Privé — What Lowtide Should Do Next

## TL;DR

**Recommendation: Option C — spin Privé into a separately branded, co-owned nightlife sibling, and protect Lowtide as the curated, B2B-anchored mother brand.** Pure Option A (kill Privé) leaves cash and community on the table and risks burning the Mlab relationship Alberto will need for talent and door credibility in Valencia. Pure Option B (run both under Lowtide's halo) is the most dangerous path: it tells Devialet, Hoka, On, and any wellness-aligned hotel that Lowtide is a club promoter who also does morning runs — which is the opposite of the wedge the Brief locks in ("run-first wellness ritual + curated daytime DJ"). C lets Lowtide stay sober-coded, daytime, sponsor-safe, while Privé becomes the underground feeder that pays cash, sharpens Alberto's selection, and keeps Mlab inside the tent. **The 90-day test is whether Mlab will agree to a clean brand wall: separate IG, separate visual system, separate booking inbox, no cross-tagging from Lowtide's grid. If they refuse, fall back to A.**

- **Mlab Valencia** `[verify]` — read as a Valencia-based DJ/party collective operating in the broader "lab"-coded underground tradition (think Paral·lel/MUTEK-adjacent in Barcelona, or smaller Madrid collectives like Goa Imports or LeFreak chapters). Likely characteristics: 6–18 person collective, rotating residents, house/techno/electro-leaning programming, venue-hopping (warehouse, tardeo, occasional club takeovers like La 3, Spook, or Cabanyal-area spaces), ticket prices €10–18 advance. Probably not currently monetised as a registered SL — operates as a brand on top of individuals' autónomo invoices.
- **Privé** `[verify]` — the joint Lowtide × Mlab format. Name signals "selected guest list" / "members feel" — i.e. the format leans clubby + curated, not ritual. Probably evening / late-night, alcohol-served by the venue, no run, no breathwork, no Devialet-grade hi-fi context. Capacity guess: 150–400 depending on venue. ### What we can infer with high confidence

- Privé is **night-coded and bar-coded** by name and by the partner (a club-leaning collective). That's not a flaw — it's just a different category from the Lowtide Brief's positioning.
- Mlab brings something Lowtide does not have organically: **a Valencia-native crowd, scene credibility with promoters, and access to venues that won't book a "wellness DJ" cold.** That's real strategic value, even if it doesn't show up on a B2B P&L.
- Lowtide brings something Mlab does not have: **production polish, brand-safe positioning, international/wellness audience, sponsor access.** This is also why Mlab will want to keep collaborating — Lowtide raises Mlab's ceiling.
- The two brands therefore have **asymmetric value flows**, which is the structural condition that almost always breaks "let's just keep doing it together" arrangements within 12–18 months unless boundaries are formalised.

## 2. The three options

### Option A — Concentrate fully on Lowtide (drop Privé / Mlab)

**Pros**
- Maximum brand clarity. Every euro of Alberto's calendar, content, and sales energy goes into the wedge the Brief locks in.
- Eliminates sponsor brand-safety questions before they're asked.
- Forces productisation discipline (Run Ritual / Sunset Residency / Corporate Reset) since there's no clubby cash flow to fall back on.

**Cons**
- Burns the Mlab relationship and the Valencia underground social capital that comes with it. In a city this size, that capital is hard to rebuild.
- Removes a useful crowd-feeder: clubby Privé attendees are exactly the 25–32 Valencia local who later buys a Lowtide ticket once their lifestyle shifts.
- Cuts off a real-time A&R surface. Privé nights are where Alberto hears the next residents and tests selections he'd never risk at a sponsor-funded daytime activation.

**Risks**
- Mlab feels dropped → quietly closes doors at venues, talent, and promoter networks.
- Alberto's calendar gets too thin in the gap between B2B closings and ticketed flagships.

### Option B — Continue Privé / Mlab collabs under the Lowtide halo

**Pros**
- No painful conversation. Cash and community continue.
- More frequent IG content surface.

**Cons (this is the dangerous option)**
- **Brand cannibalization is highest here.** A Devialet brand-safety reviewer, an On Running activation lead, a Madefor partnerships manager, a 4-star hotel F&B director — all of them will scroll Lowtide's IG once before signing. If they see club nights, alcohol, late hours, club crowds, the wellness wedge collapses. The deck says "sober-coded" — Privé contradicts that.
- The Brief explicitly refuses this: *"Alcohol-led identity (sober-coded brand, even at nightclub gigs — wellness sponsors won't activate against club-coded brands)."* B is the path that violates that rule.
- Operationally, two formats under one brand also confuses pricing. The €1,500 floor and €30 ticket cap from the Brief stop making sense the moment Privé has a €12 advance ticket on the same grid.

**Risks**
- One bad Privé night (capacity issue, noise complaint, fight, drug press coverage) becomes a Lowtide story. Reverse halo, irreversible.
- Sponsors quietly say no without telling you why — the most expensive form of failure.

### Option C — Spin Privé into a separate brand; Lowtide stays pure; Mlab becomes co-owned nightlife sibling

**Pros**
- Lowtide remains 100% on-wedge for B2B and sponsor-facing audiences.
- Privé survives, which keeps Mlab inside the tent and protects the Valencia social graph.
- Creates a **two-tier portfolio** (premium ritual / underground night) which is exactly the structure premium operators converge on (see §4). Optionality without contamination.
- Allows Privé to grow on its own economics (door + bar) without dragging Lowtide's pricing floor down.

**Cons**
- More surface to run: separate IG, separate visual, separate inbox, separate light legal layer (even if just a DBA / clear ownership memo with Mlab).
- Requires a real conversation with Mlab about ownership splits, decision rights, and who controls the IP of the Privé name.
- Slower compounding because content/audience don't pool.

**Risks**
- If brand wall isn't enforced (founders cross-tagged constantly, same photographers, same captions), C collapses into B in practice.
- Co-ownership with a collective is messy — collectives don't have one decision-maker. Need a named Mlab counterparty.

## 3. Decision framework — signals that should push you to A, B, or C

Pick the option matching **the strongest signal you can verify in the next 30 days**, not the one that feels nicest.

| Signal | Push to |
|---|---|
| Devialet / Hoka / On / a hotel partner explicitly asks about Privé during a sponsor convo | **A or C immediately**, never B |
| Mlab will agree to a clean brand wall (separate IG, no Lowtide cross-promo, named counterparty for decisions) | **C** |
| Mlab refuses brand separation or insists on co-branding under Lowtide | **A** (this is the fork) |
| Privé's last 3 nights netted < €800/night to Alberto after splits | **A** (not worth the brand risk for that economics) |
| Privé's last 3 nights netted > €1,500/night and feed real names into the Lowtide list | **C** |
| Alberto's calendar already at >70% utilization on B2B + flagships | **A** (no capacity for a sibling brand) |
| Alberto's calendar has 2+ free weekends/month and B2B pipeline is still cold | **C** (Privé is a useful cash + content stream while pipeline warms) |
| The Mlab counterparty is one named person Alberto trusts to sign things | **C** is viable |
| The Mlab counterparty is "the collective" (no single decision-maker) | **A** — co-ownership will fail |

The one signal that overrides everything: **if event 1 of the Lowtide Ritual flagship (Q3 2026) is within 90 days, freeze all Privé activity until after that flagship ships.** Sponsor-facing brand purity matters more than €X of club revenue in the same window.

## 4. Multi-brand operator case studies

Three real examples of how operators handle this exact tension. All `[verify]` against current state — these are pattern observations from training data.

### 4.1 Brunch Electronik (Barcelona / Lisbon / Madrid)
Brunch is the daytime, family-friendly, sponsor-safe outdoor brand (Estrella Damm, Movistar, mainstream press). The same operator team has also run **Brunch In The City** and **Brunch In The Park** as variants, but critically it has **kept the underground/late-night dimension out of the Brunch IP entirely** — when the same crew wants to programme harder/later, they do it under different vehicles or as bookings inside other promoters' nights, not under Brunch. The lesson: Brunch's value is its mainstream-safe halo, and they protect it ruthlessly. **This is the C model executed well.**

### 4.2 Soho House / Soho Mews / Soho Friends
Soho House is the curated members brand. They created **Soho Friends** as a lower-tier access vehicle and **High Road House / Mollie's** as adjacent hospitality experiments. They never let the cheaper / broader products use the Soho House name as the primary mark — sub-brands with separate visual identities. Why: **the premium brand's value is exclusion, and exclusion dies the moment the same logo appears on a product 3× cheaper.** This is the closest analogue to Lowtide × Privé: same founder energy, separate marks, separate doors.

### 4.3 Pacha Group → Lío / Destino / Pacha
Pacha runs a club brand, a cabaret/dinner brand (Lío), and a hotel/daytime brand (Destino). All visibly part of the same group, but each has its **own logo, own creative, own pricing tier, own programming team.** A wellness sponsor activating at Destino isn't worried about what happens at the club at 4am, because the brand wall is real. **The architecture Lowtide should copy: shared ownership, separated marks, separated calendars.**

### 4.4 (Counter-example) Daybreaker
Daybreaker stayed disciplined: it never let itself become a nighttime brand, even when chapter captains pushed for it. Result: 12+ years in, it's still sponsor-safe, still wellness-coded, still bookable by Lululemon and Equinox. The Brief already cites Daybreaker's tighter captain model as the reference — extend the same logic to format: **Lowtide is a daytime/dawn brand, full stop.** Anything else is a different brand or it isn't yours.

The convergent pattern across all four: **the curated brand never carries the underground product on its own logo.** Operators who break this rule (smaller cases — many Berlin and Madrid promoters who let their "premium" brand do warehouse parties for cash) lose the premium positioning within 24 months and can't get it back.

## 5. Brand-safety / sponsor calculus

Put yourself in the seat of a Devialet brand manager, a Hoka EMEA activations lead, a Madefor partnerships director, a Marina Beach Club F&B head deciding on a Sunset Residency.

What they Google before signing:
1. The Lowtide IG handle.
2. The founder's personal IG.
3. Press coverage of recent events.
4. Tagged photos at recent events.

**What kills the deal:**
- Visible alcohol sponsor-clash (a competing beer in the front of a recent reel).
- Late-night club crowd photography on the grid.
- Drug-adjacent visual codes (red lighting + 4am crowd shots, even if nothing happened).
- Comments / tags from accounts whose own grid is club-coded.

**Option B fails all four checks** unless Privé has been visually neutered into looking like a Lowtide product, at which point it's not Privé anymore — it's just diluted Lowtide.

**Option C passes** if and only if:
- Privé has its own handle, never tagged from @lowtide.
- Alberto's personal IG either stays neutral or is split (founder of two things is fine; founder photographed 4× a month in a club is not, regardless of brand).
- Lowtide's grid stays daytime, sober-coded, ritual-coded.
- Press for Privé goes to nightlife press (Mixmag, DJ Mag, local Valencia nightlife verticals); press for Lowtide goes to wellness/lifestyle press (Neo2, Vein, Apartamento, Condé Nast Traveler).

The Devialet "v1 → v2 → v3" sequence in the Brief is fragile by design. **Until v3 is signed, treat any sponsor-visible Privé activity as a Devialet-deal risk and price it accordingly.** A €1,200 Privé door split that costs you a 4-city Iberian Devialet partnership is the worst trade in the deck.

## 6. Recommendation — the opinionated call

**Run Option C, with conditions, and a 90-day test before you commit beyond Q3 2026.**

### The operating model that makes C work

1. **Two brands, two grids, two inboxes.** `@lowtide` and `@prive.club` (or whatever Mlab agrees to) — never cross-tagged on grid posts. Stories OK occasionally, grid posts no.
2. **Two visual systems.** Lowtide stays Mediterranean, sober-coded, daytime palette, run-first imagery. Privé takes a darker, club-coded visual — different typography, different colour, different photographer.
3. **One operating company, two project lines (or two cost centres).** Lowtide as autónomo / SL is the contracting entity. Privé runs as a co-produced line item with a written split memo with one named Mlab counterparty (not "the collective"). Revenue split documented per event.
4. **Clear pricing floors that don't conflict.** Lowtide €1,500 B2B floor and €30 ticket cap stay. Privé prices on its own logic (€12–18 advance, door splits with venue) and never gets compared on the same grid.
5. **Calendar discipline.** No Privé in the 21 days before or 7 days after a Lowtide flagship or a sponsor activation. Period.
6. **Founder voice rule.** Alberto's personal IG can claim founder status of both, but the visual ratio of posts stays at minimum 4:1 Lowtide:Privé. Wellness sponsors check personal grids.

### 90-day test plan (May → Aug 2026)

- **Weeks 1–2:** Sit down with the named Mlab counterparty. Propose the brand wall in writing. If they say no → fall back to A.
- **Weeks 3–4:** Spin up the separate Privé handle, visual system, pricing memo. Migrate any joint past content off the Lowtide grid.
- **Weeks 5–10:** Run 2 Privé nights under the new architecture. Track: net €/night to Alberto, whether any Privé attendee converts to a Lowtide ticket buyer, any sponsor-side feedback (positive or negative).
- **Weeks 11–12:** Decision gate. Continue C if (a) the brand wall held, (b) Privé netted >€1,000/night to Alberto, (c) zero sponsor red flags, (d) the Mlab counterparty was a single decision-maker who shipped on time. Else collapse to A — kindly, in person, with a one-line "Lowtide is going B2B-only for the next 12 months and I can't keep Privé going at the same time" script.

### If you have to do A — the breakup script

Short, in person, no ambiguity:

> "Lowtide is going hard on B2B and sponsor work for the next 12 months. The wellness positioning won't survive nightlife collabs on my brand, and I don't want to drag Privé into a watered-down version of itself either. I'm pulling out of co-producing Privé. I want Mlab to keep the name and run it without me. I'll still come to your nights, I'll still recommend you for bookings I can't take, but my name comes off Privé as a co-producer."

Don't apologise, don't equivocate, don't leave the door 30% open. Clean breaks preserve relationships; messy ones destroy them.

### If you have to do B (against advice)

Only acceptable form: rename Privé to a Lowtide-subordinate format like *Lowtide After Hours*, with explicit "no alcohol sponsor placement, no late-night visuals on the grid, end by 02:00, no club venues" rules. This is essentially C in disguise. If you can't accept those rules, you're in real B and you're betting the sponsor lane against the door split. That's a bad trade.

## 7. What to ask Mlab in the next 7 days

Bring these to one in-person conversation with **the named Mlab decision-maker** (not a group chat):

1. **Decision rights.** "Who on your side actually signs and decides? I need one name."
2. **Brand wall.** "Are you OK with Privé getting its own IG, its own visuals, no cross-tagging from Lowtide's grid? If not, why not?"
3. **Ownership.** "Who owns the Privé name and IP? If we split tomorrow, who keeps it? I'd like Mlab to keep it — does that work for you?"
4. **Splits.** "What's a fair revenue split per night, written down per event? Door, bar, sponsor cash separately."
5. **Calendar lock.** "Can you accept a 21-days-before / 7-days-after blackout around any Lowtide flagship or sponsor event?"
6. **Alcohol & sponsor policy.** "Privé can have a beer sponsor; Lowtide can't. Are you OK that those don't mix on either grid?"
7. **Cadence.** "What's the right cadence for Privé in 2026 — monthly, every 6 weeks, quarterly? I'm pushing for less, not more."
8. **Talent.** "Who do you want to bring through Privé in the next 90 days? I want to see the booking ambition before I commit."
9. **Press.** "Privé press goes to nightlife verticals, Lowtide press to wellness/lifestyle. Agreed?"
10. **The hard one.** "If a Devialet-tier sponsor tells me they're uncomfortable with Privé being associated with me, I will have to step back. Can you live with that risk before we go further?"

The answers Alberto gets in that conversation — particularly to **#1 and #10** — decide whether to ship C or fall back to A. There is no version of the answers that should push to B.
