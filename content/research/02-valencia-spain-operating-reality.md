## 1. Valencia regulation reality for amplified music

Valencia operates under a layered regime: state law (Ley 7/2002 sobre Contaminación Acústica), the Valencian regional Ley 7/2002 de Protección contra la Contaminación Acústica, and the **Ordenanza Municipal de Protección contra la Contaminación Acústica** of Ajuntament de València [verify]. The practical consequences for a curated DJ project:

- **Public space (plazas, beach, parks).** Amplified music in public space requires an `ocupación de vía pública` permit plus a specific `autorización por actividad musical`. These are issued case-by-case and are routinely denied in the **Ciutat Vella, Russafa and El Cabanyal ZAS zones** (Zonas Acústicamente Saturadas) [verify]. Russafa and parts of Ciutat Vella have been declared ZAS for years, which caps new licenses and tightens dB limits.
- **Decibel limits.** General municipal limits are roughly 65 dB(A) day / 55 dB(A) night at the façade of the nearest dwelling, with stricter values inside ZAS [verify]. Beachfront and industrial zones tolerate higher values.
- **Hospitality licenses (the key axis).** Spanish hospitality licenses are tiered. The relevant categories for Lowtide:
  - **Categoría 2.1 / 2.2 — Bar, cafetería, restaurante:** ambient music only, no dance floor, hard close around 00:30–01:30 [verify].
  - **Categoría 2.3 — Bar especial / pub:** allows amplified music and a small DJ booth, no dance floor, closes ~02:30 [verify].
  - **Categoría 2.4 — Discoteca / sala de fiestas:** full DJ + dance, closes 03:30–06:00 depending on subtype [verify].
  - **Café-concierto / café-teatro:** allows live performance under stricter capacity and acoustic conditions.
  Most "terrazas" and restaurants Lowtide currently plays at are 2.1/2.2 — legally they cannot host an amplified DJ set. They do it anyway under "ambientación musical" cover, which is the grey zone the Ajuntament periodically clamps down on.
- **Daytime vs nighttime.** The single most important operational lever: a **daytime event (12:00–20:00) on a licensed terrace or beach club is dramatically easier to clear** than the same event at night. Noise complaints drop, neighbour-association friction drops, and many 2.1/2.2 venues can host an "evento privado con música amplificada" daytime under a one-off `licencia de actividad ocasional`.
- **Marina de València / La Marina.** Operated by the Consorcio Valencia 2007 / Marina de València authority. Events inside the Marina perimeter (Tinglados, Edificio del Reloj, Veles e Vents) follow a separate concession regime; amplified music is permitted with an event-specific license issued by the Marina authority + Ajuntament [verify]. This is why brands run there (e.g., America's Cup-era activations, Marina Beach Club).
- **Cabanyal / Malvarrosa beachfront.** The "chiringuitos de playa" run on annual concessions from Costas + Ajuntament. They have built-in amplified-music allowance until ~midnight in summer season, but the concessions are scarce and politically sensitive. Marina Beach Club, La Más Bonita, La Pepica zone are the licensed options [verify].
- **Russafa.** Saturated. Indoor cocktail bars with 2.3 licenses can host a DJ; rooftops and terraces almost always cannot, regardless of what they currently do.
- **Hotels.** A hotel with a `licencia de hospedaje` does **not** automatically get music rights for its terrace or pool. They need either (a) a co-located 2.3/2.4 license for the F&B outlet, or (b) a `licencia de actividad ocasional` per event, capped at a small number per year per address [verify, typically 6–12/year]. This is exactly why a "DJ on the See You Hotel terrace after a 15K race" is feasible as a one-off but cannot become weekly without escalating the license.

**Bottom line for Lowtide:** the legal sweet spot is **daytime, licensed venue (2.3/2.4 or beach concession or Marina), amplified music declared upfront, ideally framed as a private/ticketed event** rather than open public access. Everything else is operating on tolerance.

## 3. Run-club + DJ scene in Spain

The "run + party + coffee" format is the fastest-growing brand-friendly motion in Spanish urban culture (2024–2026). Real cases:

- **Madrid — Madefor** (Lowtide's own anchor). Sunday run-club model with post-run DJ + coffee. Brand partners around running shoes and energy drinks [verify Madefor sponsor list].
- **Madrid — On Running club + Café Comercial / On Labs Madrid.** On opened a flagship in Madrid with run-club programming and recurring DJ-led mornings [verify].
- **Madrid — Tracksmith Pop-ups & Distrito Carrera** — premium running brand, occasional sound-design events.
- **Barcelona — Midnight Runners BCN** — global franchise, music-led group runs (boombox / live DJ on certain editions).
- **Barcelona — NBRC (Nike Bread & Butter) / Nike Run Club Barcelona** — paused/restructured but Nike continues run-music crossovers via Air Max Day events [verify].
- **Barcelona — Satisfy Running pop-ups** — French premium brand, has run BCN activations with curated music.
- **Bilbao — Bilbao Night Marathon afterparties** at BBK Live-adjacent venues; emerging but smaller.
- **San Sebastián — Behobia–San Sebastián 20K weekend** — large hospitality brands run wellness-music brunches around it; less codified than Madrid.
- **Valencia itself** — Maratón Valencia weekend (December) is the single largest annual gravity well: every premium brand activates. **This is the most strategic date in Lowtide's calendar**: Asics (title sponsor), New Balance, Hoka, On all run hospitality.

**Who pays:** activation fee is paid by the brand, not the runners. Typical structure: brand pays venue + DJ + coffee partner; the runners are free. Fee bands `[verify]`: micro-activation €1.5–4K, brand-day €5–15K, flagship anchor €20–50K.

**Sponsor archetypes in this scene:** running shoes (On, Hoka, Asics, NB, Saucony, Adidas Terrex), sportswear (Lululemon, Satisfy, District Vision), recovery (Therabody, Whoop), nutrition (Maurten, Aperol-style spritz brands for the post-run angle), audio (Devialet — see file 04 — Bose, JBL), coffee (Toma, Right Side, Nomad, Three Marks).

## 4. Sync Social Club — deep dive

**What it is `[verify]`:** Valencia-based curated experience club. Format = paid-ticket "social" sessions blending music, food, and design, oriented toward the international/expat audience that grew in València post-2021.

**What is publicly known / commonly cited (all `[verify]` since pages were not scraped):**
- Founded by a small team with hospitality + creative-direction backgrounds, Valencia-native.
- Format is "experience" rather than "club night": often early-evening, seated/standing hybrid, frequently in unusual venues (rooftops, gardens, factories).
- Pricing: ticketed at ~€20–45 per event, sometimes membership/subscription overlay.
- Audience: 28–42, international, design-aware, English-friendly. Distinctly NOT the Spanish 18–24 club crowd.
- Community model: Instagram-led, drop-style ticket sales, FOMO and curation as the moat.
- Brand partnerships: yes, but understated — drinks brands, fashion, hospitality sponsors integrated into the design rather than logo'd.

**What Lowtide can borrow:**
- Ticket-as-filter: paying €25 pre-selects an audience that respects the music.
- Unusual venue + curation as the product (not the headliner DJ).
- Bilingual / English-first communication to capture the València expat surge.
- Drop-style scarcity instead of a weekly residency.

**What Lowtide can differentiate on:**
- **Wellness/run-club vector** — Sync is design+social; Lowtide can own the body-first axis (run, swim, sauna, breathwork → sound).
- **Daytime ritual** — explicit "ritual" framing instead of "social club".
- **Music-first credibility** — Lowtide's DJ catalogue (YouTube + IG presence) is the artistic anchor that Sync deliberately keeps soft.
- **Brand pipeline** — Lowtide can sell Devialet / On / Hoka type partnerships that need a wellness frame, which Sync's social-club frame doesn't unlock.

## 6. Implications for Lowtide — recommendations

1. **Anchor the calendar on Maratón Valencia weekend (December).** Build a flagship Lowtide Ritual edition tied to the marathon: pre-race shakeout run + post-race recovery sound bath + curated DJ. This is the single highest-leverage brand-money date in Valencia.

2. **Pick two legal home venues, not ten grey ones.** Recommend: (a) one beach/marina partner with a 2.4 license (Marina Beach Club or a Cabanyal chiringuito); (b) one "factory" partner (Fábrica de Tardeo or La Fábrica de Hielo). Stop relying on 2.1/2.2 hotel terraces as recurring stages — make those one-off premium drops only.

3. **Set up the licensing entity properly.** Register Lowtide as the event organiser (autónomo or SL), get civil-liability insurance €600K–1M minimum, and learn the `licencia de actividad ocasional` workflow at Ajuntament. This unlocks hotel/terrace one-offs legally and makes brand contracts signable.

4. **Daytime-first product line.** Frame the recurring product as a daytime ritual (Sunday 12:00–18:00). Reserves the legal headroom and differentiates from Mini/Resume/Killing Time night residencies, which become guest spots, not the brand.

5. **Productise three formats, ticket two of them.** (a) Free brand-paid run-club after-party (Madefor / On / Hoka pays) — top-of-funnel. (b) €25–35 ticketed "Lowtide Ritual" curated session — Sync-style drop, sells the brand. (c) €3–10K private brand commission — corporate, hotels, product launches.

6. **Partner playbook — concrete first calls.** On Running Spain, Hoka Iberia, Lululemon Spain (wellness budget exists), Devialet (see file 04), Madefor expansion to Valencia, Sync Social Club for one collab drop ("Sync × Lowtide" — converts the SYNC × LTR dashboard storyline into a real event).

7. **Own the run-club + DJ Valencia category before Madrid operators arrive.** Madefor will eventually try Valencia; Brunch Electronik will eventually try a daytime series here. A 12-month head start with 6–8 well-executed editions and a permitted home venue is defensible.

8. **Document everything as the brand asset.** Lowtide's video/IG output is already the strongest asset (per the YouTube + IG channels listed but not scraped this session). Treat each Ritual edition as a content shoot first, an event second — that is what makes the brand sponsorable at €10K+ tiers.
