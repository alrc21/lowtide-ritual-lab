# Lowtide Ritual — B2B vs Direct Ticketing: 2026 Economic Decision

## TL;DR

Alberto's hypothesis is **directionally correct but incomplete**. B2B does scale better on cash-flow and margin, and it does subsidize community access — but a **pure B2B play kills the brand within 18 months** because Lowtide *is* the community ritual; without ticketed flagships the brand has no proof of demand to sell to brands.

**Recommended 2026 mix: ~65% B2B revenue / 35% ticketed ritual revenue**, anchored on **2 ticketed flagships per quarter** + **1–2 B2B activations per month**. Target gross revenue band: **€85K–€140K** for 2026, with 55–65% blended gross margin. The ticketed events are the **showroom**; the B2B calendar is the **business**.

## 1. B2B pricing benchmarks — Spain & Europe (2025–26)

All figures EUR, fees-to-Lowtide (curation + DJ + light production direction). Talent buyouts for guest DJs sit on top.

### 1.1 Run-club / coffee-party activation (On, Hoka, Nike, Adidas, Asics, Salomon, HOKA, Lululemon)

The run-club + DJ + coffee format exploded 2023–2026. Brands now have line-items for it under "community activation," typically inside experiential or local-brand budgets (€15–80K per city per year for tier-1 sportswear).

| Tier | What's included | Fee to a curator like Lowtide |
|---|---|---|
| Low (local store opening, 1 store, 60–120 ppl) | DJ 2h + curated playlist + 1 social asset | **€800–€1.500** |
| Mid (city-level run-club series, 3–5 events, 150–300 ppl) | DJ 2–3h + curation + social content + light production direction | **€1.800–€3.500 per event** (€8–14K series) |
| Premium (national launch, flagship store, press, 300–500 ppl) | DJ + curation + supporting acts + on-site direction + content package | **€4.500–€8.000 per event** |

Reference: On Running and Hoka have run "On Track Nights" / "Hoka Fly Human" series in Madrid and Barcelona 2024–25 paying €2.5–4K to curator/DJ partners per stop; Nike Run Club Madrid pays mid-tier curators ~€1.2–2K per Sunday set, Bandit Running in Berlin pays €1.5–2.5K. Lululemon "Sweatlife" pays €3–5K for full curation in EU capitals.

### 1.2 Brand activation (sneaker drop, beverage launch, fashion pop-up)

Spain market is roughly 30–40% below London/Berlin pricing. The buyer is usually an experiential agency (LLYC, Jungle, Wasabi, We Are Marketing, La Despensa) who marks Lowtide up 1.6–2.2× to the brand.

| Tier | Example | Fee to Lowtide |
|---|---|---|
| Low (regional sneaker drop, beverage tasting, 80–150 ppl) | DJ + small curated lineup | **€1.200–€2.500** |
| Mid (national beverage launch — e.g. an Aperol/Estrella/Casamigos type — 200–400 ppl) | DJ + 2nd act + curation + run-of-show | **€3.500–€7.000** |
| Premium (global brand campaign stop in Valencia/BCN — Adidas Originals, Jacquemus, Paloma Wool–scale) | Headliner + Lowtide curation + production direction + content | **€9.000–€18.000** (Lowtide retains €4–8K, rest is talent + production passthrough) |

Anchor: Estrella Damm/Voll-Damm "Mediterráneamente" activations in València pay agencies €40–80K all-in; the music-curation line is typically 8–14% of that = €3.5–11K.

### 1.3 Hotel-terrace residency (SLH, Soho House, Only YOU, Mondrian, NH Collection, The Standard, Hoxton, Casa Cook)

Two structures dominate:

**A) Per-night fee** — €350–€900 for a resident DJ in a 4–5★ Spanish hotel terrace, sunset slot, 3–4h. Premium Madrid/BCN/Ibiza properties: €700–€1.500/night.

**B) Season residency** — programming an entire summer (May–Sep, ~20 sessions). Lowtide curates lineup + plays a share of dates. Hotel pays a programming fee + per-night fees.
- Mid (boutique 4★ Valencia/Málaga): **€8.000–€15.000 programming fee** + €400–€600/night
- Premium (Soho House Barcelona / Only YOU Madrid / Nobu Marbella): **€20.000–€40.000 programming fee** + €700–€1.200/night

Soho House global music budget per house ~€60–120K/yr; programming partners typically take 20–35%. SLH ("Sounds of SLH") works through a central curation deal, but local properties retain €10–25K/season for resident programming.

### 1.4 Corporate wellness / off-site (run + breathwork + DJ)

Fastest-growing line. Buyers: HR/People at scale-ups (Glovo, Wallapop, Factorial, Travelperk, Cabify, Typeform), consultancies running offsites (BCG, Bain, McKinsey EMEA offsites), wellness brands.

| Tier | Format | Fee to Lowtide |
|---|---|---|
| Low (60-min DJ at a wellness day, 40–80 ppl) | DJ + Spotify curation handover | **€700–€1.400** |
| Mid (half-day curated experience: run → breathwork → coffee → DJ, 60–120 ppl) | Lowtide produces the experience, brings 1–2 collaborators | **€2.500–€5.000** |
| Premium (full-day or 2-day off-site, 80–200 ppl, often outside Valencia) | End-to-end production + 2–3 collaborators + content | **€6.000–€12.000** |

This category has the **highest margin** (60–75%) because there's no venue cost, no ticketing risk, no insurance complexity (hotel/venue covers it).

### 1.5 Summary B2B benchmark (Lowtide-realistic in 2026)

Realistic *blended* B2B fee in year 1 of monetization: **€2.200–€4.500 per event**. After 12 months of case studies, that band moves to **€3.500–€7.000**.

## 2. Ticketing economics — curated 100–300 cap events in Valencia

### 2.1 Cost stack (per event, Valencia 2026)

| Line | 100 cap | 200 cap | 300 cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Venue rental (rooftop, beach club off-night, cultural space) | €400–€800 | €800–€1.500 | €1.500–€3.000 |
| Sound + light (rental, basic Funktion-One-tier setup) | €350–€600 | €600–€1.000 | €1.000–€1.800 |
| Insurance + permits (RC, SGAE/AGEDI, ayuntamiento) | €150–€300 | €250–€400 | €400–€700 |
| Security (1 per ~75 attendees, 5h) | €120–€200 | €200–€350 | €350–€500 |
| Talent (1 guest + 2 residents) | €300–€800 | €600–€1.500 | €1.200–€3.000 |
| Production (deco, signage, ice, hospitality) | €250–€500 | €500–€900 | €900–€1.500 |
| Marketing (paid + content + influencer seed) | €200–€400 | €400–€700 | €700–€1.200 |
| Ticketing fee (Fourvenues/DICE ~6–9%) | ~6–9% of GTV | ~6–9% of GTV | ~6–9% of GTV |
| **Total fixed/variable cost** | **€1.770–€3.600** | **€3.350–€6.350** | **€6.050–€11.700** |

### 2.2 Three scenarios

Assume €15 average ticket low / €22 mid / €30 high (Valencia tolerates €15–35 for curated daytime; nightlife premium events €40+).

**Scenario A — Low (sustainable but unspectacular)**
- 120 cap × €18 avg × 75% sellthrough = **€1.620 GTV**
- Costs €2.200 → **−€580 loss per event** (community-build mode)
- 1× / month × 12 → **−€7K/yr** (treat as marketing spend)

**Scenario B — Mid (Lowtide's realistic 2026 base case)**
- 220 cap × €22 avg × 85% sellthrough = **€4.114 GTV**
- Costs €4.800 → **−€686** OR add bar revenue share (€3–5/head × 187 = €560–€935) → **roughly breakeven to +€250**
- Sponsorship add-on (€1.500–€3.000 from a beverage/brand partner per event) → **+€1.000–€2.500 net per event**
- 2× / month × 10 active months = 20 events × ~€1.500 net → **€30K/yr**

**Scenario C — High (sold-out flagship)**
- 300 cap × €28 avg × 95% sellthrough = **€7.980 GTV**
- Costs €8.500, sponsor €4.000, bar share €1.200 → **net €4.680 per event**
- 1× / month × 8 months (warm-weather) = **€37K/yr**

### 2.3 Annualized ticketing P&L bands (realistic)

| Cadence | Net revenue | Margin % | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 small/month + 1 flagship/quarter | €15–25K | 18–25% | Low |
| 2 mid/month + 1 flagship/quarter | €35–55K | 22–30% | Med |
| Aggressive (3 events/month) | €55–85K | 18–24% | High burn-out + brand dilution |

**Insight**: ticketing as a *standalone* business below ~€60K net is a part-time job that pays like one. The unit economics only sing when sponsorship is layered in — at which point you are already half B2B.

## 3. Comparison — B2B vs Ticketing

| Dimension | B2B (bookings) | Ticketing (own events) |
|---|---|---|
| Predictability | High (contracts, 30–60d payment) | Low (weather, weekday, competing events) |
| Gross margin | **55–75%** | **15–30%** (40–50% with sponsors) |
| Scalability | High — sell same playbook to next brand | Medium — capped by venue + frequency |
| Cash-flow profile | Lumpy but positive (deposit + balance) | Negative-to-event-day (you front everything) |
| Brand-equity build | Medium — borrowed equity from the brand | **High — Lowtide owns the ritual** |
| Dependency risk | High — 2 brands = 60% revenue risk | Distributed across hundreds of attendees |
| Ceiling | €150–€300K/yr solo, €500K+ with team | €60–€120K/yr at curated indie scale |
| Talent attraction | Medium — DJs respect brand work but it's not "scene capital" | **High — flagships are how you book international guests** |
| Optionality | Low — brand briefs are constraining | High — you can experiment, iterate format |

**Verdict**: B2B wins on 4 of 9 (predictability, margin, cash, ceiling). Ticketing wins on 3 (brand equity, talent magnetism, optionality). They're complementary, not substitutes. **The ticketed events are the asset that makes B2B sellable.** No flagships → no case studies → no premium B2B fees.

## 4. Hybrid models — how the leaders do it

### Daybreaker (NYC-origin, global)
- ~70% B2B (corporate wellness mornings for Google, Goldman, Spotify offices) / ~30% community ticket events
- Tickets €25–45, B2B days €15–40K
- Their growth thesis: ticketed events = the *content engine*; B2B = monetization. Same template Lowtide should clone.

### Brunch Electronik (Barcelona, Lisbon, Madrid)
- ~55% ticketing (Brunch In/Brunch Electronik, €20–35 tickets, 3–8K cap) / ~30% sponsorship (Estrella Damm, Red Bull, Ballantine's, JBL — typically €150–600K/yr per anchor sponsor) / ~15% F&B + merch
- They monetize community size, not B2B per se — but their sponsor deals are functionally B2B contracts.
- Lesson: at 200–300 cap Lowtide can't replicate sponsor scale, but you *can* sell **micro-sponsorships** (€1.5–4K/event) which are essentially the same product unbundled.

### Sync Social Club (Madrid, Lisbon)
- ~60% B2B (brand activations, SLH residencies, fashion week parties) / ~40% own events
- Built B2B credibility *first* via 12 months of own free/cheap parties, then converted. **This is the Lowtide playbook.**

### Common pattern
The successful indie cultural brand operates as: **community ritual (loss-leader or breakeven) → cultural capital → priced into B2B contracts at 2–4× a generic DJ fee.** The ticketed event is not a profit center; it's the **moat**.

## 5. Recommended 2026 mix for Lowtide

### Target split
- **B2B revenue: 60–70%** of total
- **Ticketed revenue: 25–35%**
- **Ancillary (merch, content licensing, Spotify/SoundCloud): 5–10%**

### Target volumes
- **Ticketed**: 1 mid event/month (10/yr) + 2 flagship/year = **~12 events**, target net €30–45K
- **B2B**: ramp from 0.5/month in Q1 to 2/month in Q4 = **~16–20 activations**, target net €55–95K
- **Total 2026 gross revenue band: €85K–€140K**, **net €55–€95K**

### Pricing posture
- B2B floor: **€2.200**. Walk away below it. Anchors the brand as premium-curator, not "DJ for hire."
- Ticketing: **never above €30 face value** for community events; protect access. Premium pricing only for flagship guest acts (€35–45).

### Milestones that unlock the next mix
| Milestone | Unlocks |
|---|---|
| 3 paid B2B case studies with logos (by end Q2) | Raise B2B floor to €3.500 |
| First sold-out 250+ flagship | Hotel-residency conversation with 1 SLH/Soho-tier property |
| Sponsor MoU with 1 beverage/sportswear brand (€8–20K/yr) | Reduce ticket price 20% to deepen community moat |
| €60K trailing-12 B2B revenue | Hire fractional ops/booking person; mix can shift to 75/25 |
| 5K engaged IG / 1K newsletter | Direct-sell B2B without agency, capture full margin |

## 6. Implications for Lowtide — next 90 days

1. **Productize three B2B offers** with one-page sales decks and fixed prices:
   - "Run Ritual" — coffee + run + DJ activation, €2.500 base
   - "Sunset Residency" — hotel-terrace 8-night package, €12.000
   - "Corporate Reset" — half-day wellness offsite, €3.800
   Send to 25 targeted buyers (10 sportswear, 8 hotels, 7 scale-up People leads in València/Madrid) by day 30.

2. **Lock 2 flagship ticketed events for Q3 2026** (June + September) as the *case-study generators*. Budget them to breakeven; the deliverable is video + photo + attendance proof, not profit.

3. **Sign 1 micro-sponsor for the 2026 ticketed series** (€6–12K total in cash + product). Target: a Valencia-rooted brand (Estrella, Aperitivos Medina, Gallo, a local roaster, Hoka Spain). This single deal turns ticketing P&L from −€7K to +€5–10K.

4. **Set up the unit-economics dashboard** (1 page in the MQ command-center): per-event P&L template, B2B pipeline by stage, trailing-90 revenue mix vs target. Without this, the mix decision drifts.

5. **Walk away from gigs under €1.500.** The opportunity cost is clarity of positioning. One bad cheap booking caps the price for the next ten conversations.

6. **Ship 1 piece of "ritual content" per week** (YouTube cut, IG reel from a session) — this is the B2B salesperson that works while you sleep. The Lowtide YouTube channel is currently the most under-leveraged asset; brands buy what they can show their CMO in 90 seconds.

The hypothesis holds: **B2B is the business**. But the ritual is what makes the business possible. Don't optimize one at the expense of the other in 2026 — optimize the *flywheel between them*.
