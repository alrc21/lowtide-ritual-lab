# Lowtide Ritual — Executive Brief

## Positioning (one line)

**A Valencia-born listening ritual where running, the Mediterranean, and high-fidelity sound meet — built for runners, creatives, and the international wellness traveler.**

The strategic wedge: **"run-first wellness ritual + curated daytime DJ"** — a category nobody in Spain owns yet. Madefor has run-club authority but no music curation. Sync has curation but no body axis. Brunch has scale but no intimacy. There is a clear positioning gap, and Lowtide is best placed to take it.

---

## Strategic Verdict

**B2B is the business. The ritual is what makes the business possible.** Don't optimise one at the expense of the other.

- A **pure B2B play kills the brand within 18 months** — without ticketed flagships, there's no proof of demand to sell to brands.
- A **pure ticketing play** is a part-time job that pays like one (net under €60K/yr at indie scale).
- The flywheel: ticketed events = the showroom + content engine. B2B activations = the cash. Devialet = the credibility multiplier on both.

---

## 2026 Revenue Target

| Lane | Mix | Volume | Net target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B2B activations** | 60–70% | 16–20 bookings | €55–95K |
| **Ticketed flagships + ritual** | 25–35% | 12 events (incl. 2 flagships) | €30–45K |
| **Ancillary** (merch, content, residency fees) | 5–10% | — | €5–10K |

**Gross 2026 target: €85–140K. Net: €55–95K. Blended margin 55–65%.**

**B2B floor: €1,500. Walk away below it.**
**Ticket cap: €30 face value for community events; €35–45 only for flagships with international guests.**

---

## Three Productised Formats

1. **Run Ritual** — coffee + run + DJ activation for run clubs and sportswear brands. Base price **€2,500**. Top-of-funnel, brand-paid.
2. **Sunset Residency** — 8-night curated hotel-terrace package. **€12,000**. Recurring B2B revenue.
3. **Corporate Reset** — half-day wellness offsite (run → breathwork → coffee → DJ) for HR/People at scale-ups. **€3,800**. Highest margin (60–75%).

**+ Lowtide Ritual flagship** — 2× per year, ticketed, sponsor-subsidised, the case-study generator. Q3 2026: June + September.

---

## Devialet Path

The deal sequence is **A → B → C, never D**:

- **v1 (events 1–2):** equipment loan only. Prove the format.
- **v2 (events 3–4):** loan + content rights. Editorial assets they can repost.
- **v3 (year 2):** paid partnership. Pitch a 4-city Iberian series (Valencia, Ibiza, Madrid, Lisbon).

**The single thing that decides the partnership:** event 1 must look like something Devialet would have produced themselves. Spend disproportionately on production quality at event 1, even if it loses money.

KPIs to commit: curated guest list (150 RSVP min), one editorial aftermovie, 30+ photo selects, one tier-1 media placement per flagship.
KPIs to refuse: sales conversion, HNW wallet count, UGC volume.

---

## Operating Reality (Valencia)

- Stop relying on 2.1/2.2 hotel terraces as recurring stages — make them one-off premium drops only.
- **Pick two legal home venues**: one beach/marina partner (Marina Beach Club or a Cabanyal chiringuito with a 2.4 license) + one factory partner (Fábrica de Tardeo or La Fábrica de Hielo).
- Set up the licensing entity properly: Lowtide as event organiser (autónomo or SL), civil-liability insurance €600K–1M, learn the `licencia de actividad ocasional` workflow.
- Daytime-first product line (Sunday 12:00–18:00). Reserves legal headroom and differentiates from nightclub residencies.

---

## The Anchor Date

**Maratón Valencia weekend (December)** is the single highest-leverage brand-money date in the city. Build a flagship Lowtide Ritual edition tied to it: pre-race shakeout run + post-race recovery sound bath + curated DJ. Every premium brand activates that weekend (Asics, NB, Hoka, On).

---

## 90-Day Actions

1. **Write the Lowtide Ritual arc on paper this month.** One page, minute-by-minute, 06:00–09:00 (or 18:00–21:00 for sunset). Once it exists, every gig becomes a variation, not a one-off.
2. **Productise the three B2B offers** as one-page sales decks with fixed prices. Send to 25 targeted buyers (10 sportswear, 8 hotels, 7 scale-up People leads in Valencia/Madrid).
3. **Lock 2 ticketed flagships for Q3 2026** as case-study generators. Budget to breakeven; deliverable is video + photo + attendance proof.
4. **Sign 1 micro-sponsor** for the 2026 ticketed series (€6–12K cash + product). Target: a Valencia-rooted brand (Estrella, a local roaster, Hoka Spain).
5. **Build the 10-slide Devialet deck** + lock event-1 venue + sign a media partner (Neo2, Vein, Apartamento).
6. **Walk away from gigs under €1,500.** One bad cheap booking caps the next ten conversations.
7. **Ship one piece of ritual content per week** — YouTube/IG reels are the B2B salesperson that works while you sleep.
8. **Start the email list.** "The Tide Letter" — sunrise meditations, set tracklists, run routes. The asset that survives the next algorithm shift.

---

## What Lowtide Refuses

- Alcohol-led identity (sober-coded brand, even at nightclub gigs — wellness sponsors won't activate against club-coded brands).
- Open-source / loose chapter expansion (Morning Gloryville died this way). Use Daybreaker's tighter captain model.
- Geographic expansion before product depth. Mexico is content first, chapter later (Q1 2027 earliest).
- Revenue-share deals with hardware brands (Devialet's commercial team isn't built for it).
- Festivals before a weekly/monthly Valencia residency exists.
